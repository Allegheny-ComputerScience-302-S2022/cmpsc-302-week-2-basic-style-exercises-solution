Syrian Baklava

Recipe History

Baklava is one of the most popular Middle-Eastern desserts. Its origin is widely debated where different countries prepare it differently. This is the recipe from the Levantine cuisine, specifically Syria.

Recipe Information

  • Type:Desserts
  • Author: Noor Al Mousa
  • Time:3 hrs
  • Yields:60 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 3 tsp orange blossom water
  • ¾ cup superfine or baker's sugar
  • 6 cups (1 lb., 10 oz.) chopped walnuts (pulse in a food processor for a mix of fine and somewhat coarse pieces)
  • 1 ¼ cups homemade or store-bought clarified butter (ghee or samneh), warmed until liquefied
  • 26 sheets filo dough (12 by 17 in., about 1 1/3 lbs.), thawed if frozen
  • 2 tbsp minced, lightly toasted pistachios

Directions

Make Syrup

  1. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, bring granulated sugar and 1 cup water to a boil, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add lemon juice, It helps prevent crystallization.
  3. Reduce heat to an active simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened and reduced to a scant 1 3/4 cups, 10 to 12 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat, stir in 1 tsp. orange blossom water, and let syrup cool.

Preheat oven to 350° Fahrenheit in preparation to bake.

Make Filling

  1. In a large bowl, combine superfine sugar, remaining 2 tsp. orange blossom water, and 1/3 cup syrup.
  2. Add walnuts and stir until well blended.
  3. Set remaining syrup aside.
  4. Brush a rimmed baking sheet with a bit of the butter.

Putting together Dough and Filling

  1. Unroll filo. As you work, Keep it covered with a kitchen towel if you feel it's drying.
  2. Lay 1 sheet filo in pan and brush generously with butter, making sure to cover all the dough.
  3. Repeat until you've used 14 sheets (any air bubbles that form will work their way out).
  4. Spoon filling over filo, spread evenly, and press mixture all over with back of spoon.
  5. Lay a sheet of filo on top and press it firmly with flat hands.
  6. Brush generously with butter, then repeat layering, pressing, and buttering with 5 more sheets.
  7. Layer and butter remaining 6 sheets, flattening them with your hands (no need to press).
  8. With a knife, trim shaggy filo edges flush with sides of pan (leave edges in pan for snacking later!).
  9. Using knife and a ruler, score filo at opposite long ends to mark 6 equal strips; then cut along score lines, using the ruler as a guide.
  10. Repeat on a diagonal, making 12 strips 1 1/2 in. wide to create diamonds.

Baking

  1. Bake in 350° Fahrenheit oven, rotating pan twice for even browning, until filo is deep golden and crisp, including down into cuts, about 35 minutes.
  2. Using a soup spoon, evenly coat hot baklava with 1 to 1 1/4 cups of reserved, room-temperature syrup. (How much syrup is a matter of taste)
  3. Note: If the baklava has cooled or the syrup is hot, it won't be crunchy, it will be soggy.
  4. Sprinkle center of each diamond with a generous pinch of pistachios.
  5. Let baklava cool in pan on a rack at least 1 hour.
  6. Re-cut if needed before serving, and cut bigger pieces in half if you like.
